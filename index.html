Ego's Games

game one: level

Objective: achieving highest level
Motive: proving yourself that you're good

A: Perfection

Belief: self-worth attached to expectations, either their own or others (friends, family, coach etc.)
Objective: achieving perfection
Motive: need to proof yourself
Obstacles:
  • external:
 unable to fulfilling gap between perfect expectations and reality
  • internal:
 self-criticism for not being perfect enough, that leads to overcompensating trying hard

B: Competition

Belief: "I'm better than you", measuring self-worth by comparison to others
Objective: being the best, winning, beating competition
Motive: achiving the top, need to be admired, need to control everything
Obstacles:
  • external:
 you can always find somone who will be playing even better
  • internal:
 self-worth roller coaster due to always changing win - lose nature of games, fear of losing, mind absorbed by comparison to others, what blocks resources (creativity, spontaneous etc.)

C: Image

Belief: being center of attention is most important
Objective: present yourself well
Motive: need for attention, desire to be praised
Obstacles:
  • external:
 there is no way to please everyone, some people will love you, others will hate you
  • internal:
 confused about his own identity, fear of not filling expectation of others, fear of loneliness

game two: friendship

Objective: making or maintaining friends
Motive: need for frienship

A: Status

Belief: Level is not important, it's all about where and with whom you play
Objective: raising or maintaining social status
Motive: need for friendship of high status people
Obstacles:
  • external:
 chasing standards of others
  • internal:
 fear of losing status

B: Community

Belief: All my good friends do this, therefore I need to do this to be more often around them
Objective: maintaining friendship
Motive: need for acceptance and friendship
Obstacles:
  • external:
 finding time, place and friends
  • internal:
 fear of missing out, fear of not being part of the herd

C: Spouse

Belief: doing everything to just be around that person
Objective: spending more time with object of affection
Motive: loneliness
Obstacles:
  • external:
 skills required to be able to play together
  • internal:
 self-doubt, confusion whether this is a proper way to being closer to each other, and overcoming loneliness

game three: hobby

Objective: playing for pleasure, mental focus and well-being
Motive: well being and having fun

A: Well-being

Belief: it is good to practice from time to time
Objective: mental clarity, feel emotions
Motive: feel alive, feel young, feel still relevant, mental sharpness
Obstacles:
  • external:
 finding someone with similar motivation to play with
  • internal:
 doubts about whether the game really affects the well-being, temptation to start playing for results

B: Fun

Belief: I do not play for results or improvement
Objective: experience as much pure fun from the game as possible
Motive: having fun
Obstacles:
  • external:
 none
  • internal:
 involve ego into the game

C: Learning

Belief: playing to experience flow, which allows natural learning and improvement
Objective: progress
Motive: enjoyment from learning
Obstacles:
  • external:
 none
  • internal:
 temptation to engage ego into the game
